1. Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

## ----------------------------------------------------------------------

Probability = chance of something happening




### **Definition of AI**

Machines or software that **think, learn, and make decisions** like humans.



### **Types of AI**

* **Narrow AI:** Specific task (e.g., Siri, spam filter)
* **General AI:** Human-level intelligence, multitasking
* **Super AI:** Beyond human intelligence, hypothetical


### **AI vs ML vs DL**

* **AI:** Broad concept of intelligent machines
* **ML:** Machines **learn patterns from data**
* **DL:** ML subset using **neural networks**, handles complex data like images/audio


### **Applications of AI in Industry**

* Healthcare: diagnosis, drug discovery 🏥
* Finance: fraud detection, trading 💰
* Manufacturing: predictive maintenance, robotics 🤖
* Others: self-driving cars, recommendation systems 🚗



### **Evolution of AI Technologies**

* 1950s: Rule-based systems 📝
* 1980s-2000s: Machine Learning & Expert Systems
* 2010s+: Deep Learning, Neural Networks, modern AI applications

2. Fundamentals of Machine Learning

## ----------------------------------------------------------------------
Probability = chance of something happening



Supervised Learning: Uses labeled data to predict outputs.
Unsupervised Learning: Finds patterns in unlabeled data.
Reinforcement Learning: Learns via rewards/penalties from environment.
Training vs Testing Data: Train to learn, test to evaluate generalization.
Model Evaluation Metrics: Accuracy, precision, recall, F1-score measure performance.

Neural networks ----> are layered systems that learn patterns using weighted inputs and outputs.

Activation functions---> add non-linearity and decide neuron output.(ReLU, Sigmoid, Tanh.)

Measures **error** between predicted and actual output.* Guides the model on how wrong it is.(MSE (Mean Squared Error))

Backpropagation--->Updates weights by sending error backward.(Uses chain rule (calculus).)

Gradient descent -->updates weights to reduce loss step by step.(Types: Batch, Stochastic, Mini-batch.)
